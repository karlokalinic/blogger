# Public Opinion, Class and Credibility

Karlytta’s credibility does not split cleanly by ideology. It divides through class, media habit, age, aesthetics, gender expectations and experience with institutions.

## Loyal entertainment public

They know her career and resent outsiders discovering seriousness in music they enjoyed first.

> We heard her before the critics did. Do not arrive now and explain our memory.

## Institutional conservatives

Their loyalty is to procedural continuity more than Varda personally.

> If every collaboration can later be called coercion, public life becomes impossible.

## Educated cultural skeptics

Some dislike Karlytta aesthetically and defend her legally. Others treat aesthetic contempt as an evidentiary shortcut.

## Younger archive culture

They compare files, metadata and edits. They preserve what institutions remove and can turn a checksum into theology within twenty minutes.

## Healthcare workers

They respond more strongly to access logs than lyric debates. Bureaucratic coercion is immediately legible to them.

## Credibility paradoxes

She is considered less credible because she is theatrical, although theatre was how she became audible. She is considered too powerful to be coerced, although the alleged system created and controlled parts of that power. Continuing collaboration is treated as consent even when the complaint describes access and livelihood as leverage.

Public opinion must always display question wording, sample, broadcaster, timing, payment model and the event immediately preceding the survey.
