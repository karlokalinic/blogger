# Prudina Archive Reliability Manual

Every artifact receives five scores from 0 to 4.

- **Provenance:** unknown origin to verified custody.
- **Integrity:** visibly altered to preserved original.
- **Access bias:** one interested custodian to several competing custodians.
- **Interpretive load:** direct operational fact to primarily symbolic reading.
- **Human residue:** sterile copy to material trace that changes meaning.

## Classes

**A:** operationally dependable  
**B:** useful with documented limitations  
**C:** contested but materially significant  
**D:** primarily interpretive  
**E:** likely false, fabricated or impossible to verify

False artifacts remain when their circulation caused real consequences.

## Required metadata

Title, creator, custodian, creation date, publication date, transformations, related claims, related people, contradictions, removal attempts, public availability, game relevance and privacy risk.

A technically authentic document may be cruel, misleading or irrelevant. A damaged fan copy may preserve the only surviving trace of a real event.
