# Studio North Session Log: KARLYTTA, 2014

**Session:** K14-SELF-07  
**Room:** Studio North B  
**Producer:** Dorian Belić  
**Engineer:** Sven Rasto

## 19:10 — Arrival

Karlytta arrives without an assistant and asks whether “the low voice” has already been recorded. Belić says it is only a guide. Tea: black, honey unopened, two room-temperature waters with paper covers.

## 19:36 — Chorus comp

Tracks include LEAD_A, LEAD_AIR, LEAD_CHILD, LEAD_FLAT, DOUBLE_L, DOUBLE_R, GUIDE_M, ROOM_B and CROWD_TEMP.

Beside LEAD_CHILD: **less sentence, more sound**. Another hand adds: **she hates this note**.

## 20:22 — Disagreement

Karlytta asks to preserve the full spoken refusal before the drop. Belić says it delays the hook. Official notes call the disagreement “arrangement clarification.” Sven writes privately:

> K says the no has to remain long enough to be embarrassing.

## 21:04 — Male guide

A session vocalist records approval phrases in a low register, instructed to sound certain but not angry. “To je tako” receives nine takes. Take 6 is **TOO HUMAN**. Take 8 is **STATE**.

Public paperwork lists no male session vocalist.

## 22:11 — Processing

Karlytta requests more processing on the childish phrase and less on the survival refrain.

> The small one must sound made. The prayer must sound caught.

## 23:48 — Return

Room B is opened after the official session closes. A 32-bit export is created at 01:13. The invoice lists an extra technical hour without performer names.

This log shows disagreement, editing and undocumented material. It does not prove the 2008 allegation. Its relevance is the conversion of refusal into arrangement and authority into an aesthetic layer.
