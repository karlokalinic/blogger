# Preliminary Hearing: Authentication and Scope

**JUDGE ARVEN:** Counsel, separate the song from the file. One is a cultural work. The other is a digital object with custody history. Your briefs move between them.

**LEA PAŽIĆ:** Because the defendants moved between them first. They treated the recording as entertainment when monetizing it, as political speech when licensing it and as unreliable art when preservation became inconvenient.

**DEFENCE:** That is rhetoric.

**JUDGE:** It is also a sentence. Answer it.

### Extended file

**DEFENCE:** It arrived through an anonymous account. No source device is verified.

**PAŽIĆ:** Three removal notices claim ownership and identify three different master codes.

**DEFENCE:** Automated notices do not authenticate audio.

**PAŽIĆ:** Agreed. They make the position that no related master exists unstable.

**JUDGE:** Existence and authenticity remain separate.

### Lyrics

**DEFENCE:** Plaintiff wants vague lyrics treated as contemporaneous reports.

**PAŽIĆ:** No. They are context, career history and evidence that the later account did not emerge in a thematic vacuum.

**JUDGE:** Consistency is not corroboration.

**PAŽIĆ:** Agreed.

### Preservation order

The court orders preservation of RP raw footage, North House access records, appearance-contract correspondence, ownership notices for both audio files, clinic access logs and communications concerning the phrase **national daughter**.

The court does not authenticate NOĆNI ZAPIS and does not admit song lyrics as proof of the alleged encounter.

**JUDGE:** Public figures face legitimate scrutiny. That does not create a public easement through the body, medical file or private communications.
