# KARLYTTA: Childhood Oral History

This document preserves contradictory recollections. None is designated as definitive.

## Former teacher, Mira Koš

“She was not the best singer. She was the child who continued after everybody else stopped. If the tape jammed, somebody laughed or a teacher entered, she continued. I called it discipline. Now I think she could not bear the room changing without her permission.”

Mira keeps a notebook containing the spelling **Karlyta**. The second T appears later in different ink. She says Karla added it. A classmate insists the teacher did because the first spelling looked unfinished.

## Neighbor, Savo Lukan

“She made tickets for the stairwell. Ten steps was cheap. Beside the radiator was VIP because it was warm. She charged buttons. Her mother made her return them.”

Anniversary specials call this her first residency. Savo calls it a winter outage in an apartment block.

## Childhood friend, Ena Bris

“She was generous if everybody admitted she had been generous. She gave you her favorite thing and then told the school you had it. Return it and she cried. Keep it and she said you stole it. She could make a gift into a country.”

Ena refuses television appearances. Her old comments are repeatedly paraphrased without permission.

## Choir director, Father Viren

“She understood call and response before she understood belief. She liked that a room answered one person. She disliked that the answer was prescribed.”

A parish log calls her changed refrain a disruption. A museum label later calls it improvisation.

## Local technician, Deni Pal

“The first-broadcast myth is wrong. The cable failed. But after the failure, the host removed the microphone because the schedule was ending. The accident and the human decision became one memory.”

This distinction defines Prudina. Systems do not need to plan every injury. They need routines that decide whose interruption matters.

## Karlytta, age thirty-nine

“I do not remember being talented. I remember being available.”

The line may describe opportunity, neglect, labor, ambition, vulnerability or an adult reconstruction of childhood. It should remain unresolved.
