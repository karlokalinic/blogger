# RP Network Editorial Stylebook: Restricted Extract

## Naming

Use **Karlytta** on first reference. Thereafter use **the singer** or **the plaintiff**, depending on framing. Avoid **victim** before adjudication. Avoid **accuser** in headlines because audience testing indicates that it reduces perceived neutrality.

Use **President Varda** in news copy. Use **Ognjen Varda** in legal graphics. Do not call him **the defendant** unless every named party is described by procedural status.

## Allegations

Preferred verbs: alleges, states in the filing, describes, identifies, claims. Avoid reveals, admits, confesses and finally says.

Do not write “alleged distress.” Describe visible behavior and attribute interpretation.

## Archive footage

When using older distress footage:

- show the original date;
- do not describe it as proof;
- cut prolonged silence unless editorially necessary;
- avoid angles showing staff entering frame;
- retain applause when available to prevent misleading tonal isolation.

## Music

Lyrics may be quoted for criticism and reporting, but no more than two consecutive lines without clearance. Call the longer file **the purported extended version** on first reference. Do not call it original, uncut or leaked unless attributed.

## Balance

Every segment longer than seven minutes must include one legal caution, one supportive reading, one skeptical reading, one institutional response and one audience-participation item. Balance is measured by category inclusion, not time.

## Reality programming

Pending litigation may be discussed if reenactments are labeled, guests do not state allegations as fact, voting options do not imply guilt and sponsors approve adjacent content. **WE WILL NOT SPECULATE** is cleared as a programme title.

## Internal principle

Neutrality is not the absence of framing. It is the consistent application of approved framing.
