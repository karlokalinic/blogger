# Public Legal Outline: Karlytta v. Varda et al.

This is a narrative-development document, not real legal advice.

## Defendants

1. Ognjen Varda, in personal capacity for alleged acts.
2. Office of the President, for institutional conduct and records.
3. RP Network, for alleged retaliation, privacy violations, editing and rights exploitation.
4. Varda Cultural Foundation, for contracts and intermediary pressure.

## Causes of action used in the fiction

- sexual battery / non-consensual sexual contact;
- coercive control and abuse of authority;
- invasion of privacy and unlawful record access;
- misappropriation of voice and likeness;
- retaliation and interference with professional relations;
- defamation through knowingly misleading edits;
- spoliation or concealment of relevant records;
- civil conspiracy among specific actors.

## Evidentiary spine

The songs are context, not the evidentiary spine.

The spine is:
- access logs;
- travel and security records;
- edit decision lists;
- witness logistics;
- contract threats;
- ownership notices;
- archived raw footage;
- medical privacy records;
- and contemporaneous messages.

The tracks matter because they establish recurring artistic structures, public reception and the possibility that apparently new allegations existed as long-term expression. They should never magically prove the event.

## Best dramatic legal obstacles

- limitation periods and delayed disclosure;
- immunity and distinction between personal and official acts;
- authentication of the extended recording;
- chain of custody;
- sealed medical information;
- witnesses financially dependent on defendants;
- contradictory memories;
- prior public praise of the accused;
- continued collaboration after alleged abuse;
- and the difference between profitable participation and free consent.

## Ethical rule

Do not make the case deep by making the abuse more graphic. Make it deep by tracing how ordinary systems transform refusal into scheduling, distress into content, access into protection and survival into consent.
