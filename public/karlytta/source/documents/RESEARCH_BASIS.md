# Research Basis and Design Translation

This package uses research as a structural tool, not as decorative authority. The visible fiction never names its real-world inspirations. It translates methods and pressures into Prudina's institutions.

## 1. Transmedia as dispersed knowledge

Henry Jenkins describes transmedia worlds as systems where separate media add distinct information and where audiences assemble partial knowledge collectively. The useful lesson for Slegnuće is not “put lore everywhere.” It is: each medium must do something the game cannot do as efficiently.

**Translation into the project**
- The game owns embodiment, risk, space, silence and irreversible action.
- The website owns public memory, competing interpretation and searchable contradiction.
- Songs own emotional compression and ambiguity.
- Legal files own procedural violence.
- Forum posts own collective meaning-making.
- A television archive owns the conversion of pain into spectacle.

Source: Henry Jenkins, “Transmedia Storytelling 101,” 2007.

## 2. Indexical environmental storytelling

Clara Fernández-Vara’s framing of indexical storytelling focuses on traces, indications and player inference. A trace is strongest when it is caused by an action and still bears the shape of that action.

**Translation**
A generic newspaper explaining that Karlytta was controlled is weak. A corridor plan with one door ordered locked, a microphone log signed after closing, or an edit list removing one refusal are stronger. They make the institution legible through residue.

Source: GDC, “Environmental Storytelling: Indices and the Art of Leaving Traces.”

## 3. Deduction without abolishing interpretation

Jon Ingold’s discussion of proof in detective games is useful because the Karlytta archive should never reduce to a single “correct lore answer.” The reader must distinguish:
- fact,
- allegation,
- interpretation,
- inference,
- propaganda,
- coincidence,
- and deliberate fabrication.

**Translation**
The site uses claim statuses rather than a single truth meter. Some questions can be resolved. Others should become more precise rather than disappear.

Source: GDC, “The Burden of Proof: Narrative Deduction Mechanics for Detective Games.”

## 4. Crisis communication and institutional trust

WHO guidance treats risk communication as real-time exchange that enables people to act, and community engagement as partnership rather than one-way instruction. Prudina’s media system becomes frightening when it imitates the language of public safety while denying people usable uncertainty.

**Translation**
Radio Prudina does not need to publish obvious lies. It can:
- delay operational information,
- replace uncertainty with reassurance,
- treat criticism as panic,
- present audience participation without shared power,
- and make every message emotionally complete but practically useless.

Source: World Health Organization, “Risk communication and community engagement.”

## 5. Reality and fiction as unstable categories

Theatre research on the collapsing boundary between real and fictional experience supports the central Karlytta effect: songs, staged appearances, legal testimony and public persona contaminate one another. The audience is never allowed the clean comfort of deciding that performance is unreal or that confession is unperformed.

**Translation**
Karlytta may be sincere while performing, strategic while telling the truth, vain while endangered, funny while describing harm and contradictory without being false.

Source: Erika Fischer-Lichte, “Reality and Fiction in Contemporary Theatre.”

## 6. Narrative medicine and its limits

Narrative medicine emphasizes attention to the stories surrounding illness and care. Critiques of narrativity warn that not every person experiences life as a coherent story and that forcing coherence can become another form of control.

**Translation**
The Doctor’s flaw is not that he ignores stories. It is that he wants every story to become diagnostically useful. Karlytta’s archive teaches the player that testimony can remain fragmented without becoming meaningless.

Sources: narrative-medicine literature and critiques of compulsory coherence.

## Operational rules derived from the research

1. **Every medium must add, not repeat.**
2. **Every important claim needs a trace, not merely an explanation.**
3. **Every trace has provenance, an interested custodian and a possible failure mode.**
4. **Ambiguity is designed by separating answerable questions from interpretive ones.**
5. **Institutions are shown through routines, forms, queues, edits, access rights and maintenance.**
6. **Public crises alter ordinary logistics before they produce speeches.**
7. **No character exists only to represent an ideology. Give each person a job, dependency, private cost and practical competence.**
8. **The site may deepen the world but cannot contain information required to understand the game’s main emotional arc.**
