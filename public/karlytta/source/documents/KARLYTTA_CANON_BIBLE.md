# KARLYTTA: Canon Bible v0.1

## Core sentence

Karlytta is the most publicly visible person in Prudina and one of its least successfully heard citizens.

She is not merely a singer with political opinions. She is a civic instrument, a private person, a commercial product, a recurring national emergency, a joke, a witness, an employer, a survivor, a difficult colleague and an archive that has learned to speak before anyone gives it permission.

## Identity

**Stage name:** KARLYTTA  
**Legal name:** Karla Vita Lukan  
**Born:** 18 February 1987, Prudina  
**Occupation:** singer, songwriter, performer, occasional actress, producer, television personality, patron and plaintiff  
**Public career:** 2003–present  
**Trademark:** the doubled T, registered after several early labels printed her name inconsistently  
**Signature contradiction:** she speaks as if nothing can embarrass her, then builds entire eras around what the public refused to notice.

Karlytta’s childhood should never be narrated as a prophecy. She was not born already symbolic. She was loud, observant, competitive, frightened by hospitals, obsessed with adults who could make rooms change by entering them, and unusually good at imitating the rhythm of speech before understanding the content. She learned early that performance could interrupt conflict at home without resolving it. Adults called this charm. She understood it as timing.

Her first surviving broadcast matters because the backing track fails. She continues without accompaniment, but the host lowers her microphone to regain control of the programme. Fans later treat this as the first Karlytta myth: the child whose voice survived the machine. The raw recording is less clean. She misses notes, grows angry and keeps singing partly because she does not know what else to do. That imperfection is essential. A national symbol must first be allowed to have been a child rather than a fully authored metaphor.

## The public phenomenon

Karlytta is known across class, age, ideology and region because different groups use her for incompatible purposes.

- The state uses her reach.
- Critics use her as a boundary marker for serious culture.
- Fans use her contradictions as permission to survive their own.
- Advertisers use her recognizability.
- Political movements quote her because her lines already sound remembered.
- Television uses her volatility to produce events.
- Institutions invite her when they need popularity and exclude her when they distribute legitimacy.
- Young artists inherit the market she created while being told they have replaced her.
- People who dislike her still know enough to participate in arguments about her.

Her true national power is not that everyone agrees with her. It is that almost everyone has already prepared an interpretation.

## Artistic grammar

Karlytta’s catalogue crosses club music, turbo-folk structures, opera, jazz, electronic experiment, hardstyle, ballad, spoken theatre and ceremonial music. Genre is not evidence of inconsistency. It is her method of crossing social checkpoints.

Her repeated devices are:

1. **Repetition that changes ownership.**  
   A phrase begins as her assertion, becomes the male voice’s command, becomes the crowd’s chant and returns as a memory she can no longer isolate.

2. **Childlike language inside adult machinery.**  
   “Me no likey” is grammatically reduced, catchy and apparently comic. Against a low authoritative response, it becomes a refusal that the arrangement refuses to recognize as sufficient.

3. **Quantified existence.**  
   The author-supplied fragment “Bože, samo neka me ima još… nek me ima sto” moves from survival to multiplication. She does not ask to live well. She asks to remain present in enough copies that erasure becomes administratively difficult.

4. **Bodies treated as systems.**  
   Voice, skin, shoes, breath, nerves, medication, floors, clocks, contracts and signals recur because power reaches people through procedures before it reaches them through ideology.

5. **Pleasure carrying incompatible information.**  
   The song must remain enjoyable. If the arrangement merely announces that something is wrong, the audience is morally protected. The stronger design lets people dance, quote and laugh before the context changes what they remember doing.

6. **False simplicity.**  
   Karlytta’s strongest lines can be dismissed as empty because they are structurally easy to remember. Memorability is precisely what allows them to return during political or private crises.

## The two audio objects

### Public master: KARLYTTA

The official file is 4:57, 44.1 kHz, stereo, 16-bit PCM. Computational analysis estimates an F-minor centre and a pulse around 129 BPM. It is very loud by contemporary standards, with a narrow loudness range. This is not a moral flaw. It makes the song feel continuous, public and difficult to escape.

In fiction, this is the version released, broadcast, licensed and treated as the stable cultural object. Its legal ownership is apparently simple until the lawsuit.

### Disputed source: KARLYTTA: NOĆNI ZAPIS

The extended file is 5:06, 44.1 kHz, stereo, 32-bit PCM. It is slightly louder and denser, with a darker average spectral profile and a different estimated pulse. Coarse alignment indicates that it is not merely the public master with an extra ending. Material has been re-timed or rearranged.

Its strongest canonical status is **disputed but materially real**.

RP Network calls it an unauthorized reconstruction. Producer Dorian Belić calls it a fan edit assembled from circulating stems. Former engineer Sven Rasto says it resembles a late studio export. Karlytta’s legal team refuses to authenticate it while citing the network’s inconsistent ownership notices.

Karlytta posts one sentence:

> Nije produžena. Skraćena je bila ona koju znate.

The sentence does not authenticate the file. It destroys the emotional authority of the word “extended.”

## The lawsuit

### Caption

**Karla Vita Lukan, professionally known as Karlytta  
v.  
Ognjen Varda; Office of the President; RP Network; and Varda Cultural Foundation**

### What she alleges

Karlytta alleges a specific coerced sexual encounter after a restricted state performance in 2008. She states that she had refused a private continuation of the evening, that her refusal was treated as a scheduling problem rather than an answer, and that President Ognjen Varda used political, professional and physical authority to obtain sexual access without freely given consent.

The complaint then widens. It alleges a pattern of coercive control rather than a single sealed event:

- invitations that functioned as orders;
- security practices that isolated her while remaining officially protective;
- threatened cancellations and broadcast exclusions;
- targeted tax and licensing pressure after refusals;
- monitoring of travel, relationships and medical appointments;
- unauthorized access to private records;
- forced or strategically unavoidable appearances;
- appropriation of her voice and image for political continuity campaigns;
- selective editing designed to make distress look like vanity or instability;
- and retaliation against employees who preserved unedited material.

The case is deep because it remains legally concrete while culturally expansive. Karlytta is not suing a metaphor. She names acts, dates, institutions and people. The archive then asks how an entire culture helped those acts remain visible but illegible.

### What she does not claim

She does not claim every song is literal autobiography.  
She does not claim every collaborator understood the context.  
She does not claim popularity made her powerless in every situation.  
She does not claim to have behaved consistently, graciously or wisely.  
She does not claim the public could have convicted anyone from a chorus.

Her central statement is narrower and more disturbing:

> Pisala sam to. Govorila sam to. Pjevala sam to cijeli život. Sve je bilo vani. Samo nitko nije slušao.

The statement accuses the culture of classification. People heard the sounds. They classified them as camp, trash, scandal, provocation, marketing, femininity, madness or entertainment. The failure was not physical audibility. It was the refusal to grant certain forms of speech the status of information.

## Why the male voice matters

The low male voice should not secretly be a recording of the President. That solution is lurid, implausibly convenient and dramatically smaller than the system.

The male voice is performed by a session vocalist under producer direction. Its importance is formal. It validates, corrects and approves. Karlytta’s processed high register carries reduced grammar and apparent helplessness. The low voice responds with certainty: this is how it is; this is how it should be done.

The song therefore stages a structure recognizable across romance, studio labour, bureaucracy and politics:

1. she states discomfort;
2. authority translates discomfort into style;
3. the beat rewards the translation;
4. the audience receives the exchange as pleasure;
5. repetition converts the imbalance into normal form.

This does not make the track a confession in code. It makes it an artistic structure that later becomes impossible to hear innocently.

## Karlytta's flaws

Do not protect her from moral complexity.

She humiliates assistants when frightened.  
She has used private friendships for public leverage.  
She rewrites anecdotes until the rewrite becomes her memory.  
She can recognize manipulation while practicing it.  
She sometimes withholds solidarity from younger women she perceives as replacements.  
She knows how to trigger press attention and occasionally does so irresponsibly.  
She has accepted state honours after privately describing the same officials as dangerous.  
She has benefited materially from a media system she now accuses.  
She can be cruel, vain, funny, perceptive and wrong within the same conversation.

These traits do not weaken the allegation. They prevent the narrative from requiring perfect victimhood as an admission ticket to being believed.

## Career architecture

### PRVA

The breakthrough era. Bright, direct and marketed as instinct without intellect. Later critics identify the first appearance of her core grammar: firstness as both triumph and abandonment.

### FRIENDLY STAKES

A jazz-pop television special staged around a poker table. Its surface is adult glamour. Its dramatic engine is the unequal value of risk: wealthy guests lose money; Karlytta risks reputation, access and physical safety. The special becomes cult material because nobody agrees whether the tension was scripted.

### KARLYTTA

The self-titled identity record. Publicly read as narcissistic coronation. After the lawsuit, the title is reinterpreted as an attempt to attach a fixed legal name to a voice constantly owned by others.

### FORT UMBRALIS

A large symphonic work commissioned by institutions eager to display cultural ambition. It premieres once. Officially it enters technical revision. Unofficially, a spoken section about walls that protect by preventing exit becomes politically inconvenient.

### MEMORIA MARIS

An opera-film that finally grants Karlytta institutional seriousness. The cruelty is that critics praise her only after she adopts forms they already know how to respect. Karlytta later says the sea was useful because audiences permit water to carry grief they would reject from a woman.

### ZRAKOPERKA

A futurist record released during the Third War. Emergency broadcasters use one chorus as continuity music. The state converts a work about bodies becoming instruments into proof of national resilience. This becomes a major copyright and moral dispute years before the lawsuit.

### ANESTEZIJA

The late-career body record: medication, clocks, procedures, consent, interrupted signals and the fantasy that pain becomes harmless when professionally named. This is the era most directly connected to the Doctor’s thematic world, but it remains optional to the game plot.

## Institutions around her

### RP Network

Not a cartoon ministry of lies. It is a technically competent, commercially successful and legally hybrid network that performs public service, entertainment, emergency communication, advertising and political continuity at once.

Its power comes from:
- owning the archive;
- controlling which version is easy to find;
- turning every event into a format;
- creating apparent plurality through internally managed debate;
- and ending unresolved questions at the scheduled time.

### NARODNA KUĆA

A reality institution more than a programme. Contestants live under continuous observation. Politicians, celebrities, doctors, clerics, criminals and ordinary residents appear in themed specials. Public voting is treated as democratic participation even when the available choices are produced in advance.

The show does not replace all government. It replaces the emotional work government fails to perform:
- mourning,
- punishment,
- confession,
- reconciliation,
- charity,
- public diagnosis,
- and the manufacture of common enemies.

### Office of the President

Its language is continuity. Every private demand becomes a security requirement, every security requirement becomes procedure, and every procedure becomes nobody’s personal decision.

### The courts

The courts must remain capable of meaningful resistance. A world where every institution is wholly fake is dramatically lazy and politically childish. Judges disagree. Clerks preserve documents. Procedure can protect and suffocate. The case is dangerous precisely because law may work partially.

## Relationship to the Doctor's main game

Karlytta is not secretly the key to the disease, the war, the protagonist’s family and every locked room. That would turn the world into a theme park built around one celebrity.

Use exactly one mandatory bridge and several optional echoes.

### Mandatory bridge

The Doctor encounters an access badge or audit record showing that a non-clinical RP Network account opened a protected patient file during a night shift. Karlytta’s name may be visible, obscured or replaced by a patient code depending on how direct the game should be.

The bridge serves the Doctor’s story:
- Does he report unauthorized access when doing so may expose his own procedural breach?
- Does he treat privacy as care or as an obstacle to information?
- Does he recognize that institutions can harm without falsifying medical data?

### Optional echoes

A waiting-room television runs lawsuit coverage.  
A patient quotes a Karlytta lyric incorrectly.  
A discarded concert ticket establishes a date.  
A colleague dismisses the case as celebrity noise.  
A family member knows every detail and none of the evidence.  
A clinic corridor uses a continuity song from ZRAKOPERKA during an emergency drill.

None of these must explain Karlytta. They reveal the characters who react to her.

## Canon control

Every new Karlytta artifact must include:

- **Claim:** What does this object suggest?
- **Custodian:** Who preserved or published it?
- **Interest:** What does that custodian gain?
- **Failure mode:** How could the object mislead?
- **Sensory trace:** What physical or technical detail makes it feel used?
- **Contradiction:** Which existing item resists its interpretation?
- **Game relevance:** 0 optional, 1 atmospheric, 2 thematic, 3 playable.
- **Status:** confirmed, alleged, disputed, false, unknown or performative.
- **Link:** At least one person, one event and one other artifact.

This prevents lore from becoming a pile of facts with expensive fonts.
